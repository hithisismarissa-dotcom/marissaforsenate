Marissa Emanuele - Exploring a Run for NH Senate

Hi I'm Marissa, a new mom and average person that lives in New Hampshire

I'm exploring a run for Senate

To be honest, I really don't want this job. I'm hoping this will convince one of you to run.

I'm a working mom who intends to build her family's life in New Hampshire. I'm a public relations and social media consultant with 10+ years in the tech industry, a deeply unserious TikTok personality, and Christian who thinks Jesus is probably pretty upset about how we treat each other.

I love my life here in the Granite State and have no interest in spending much of the year in swampy DC with corrupt politicians. But if our best option is going to be Chris Pappas, I'll get my bags packed. Because my neighbors and the hardworking people of New Hampshire deserve someone who cares about their future.

What I'm trying to do:

I want to collect 3,000 emails from New Hampshire voters who are sick of our politicians being bought by corporations and outside interests. I'm trying to prove that New Hampshire people want to vote for a candidate that actually supports these common-sense ideas:

1. True Personal Freedom

Government has no business in your bedroom, your doctor's office, or your personal choices. Full reproductive rights, marriage equality, end the war on drugs, and treat addiction like the health crisis it is. If it's not hurting anyone else, it's not the government's job to regulate it.

2. Economic Justice That Actually Works

Tax billionaires and corporations to fund housing, healthcare, and education for everyone. Chris Pappas talks progressive but takes corporate money - we need someone who'll actually make the wealthy pay their fair share so working class people can afford to live here.

3. Democracy Without Corporate Puppets

End the influence of PACs, AIPAC, and corporate donors completely. Pappas (along with most of our federal representation) votes to protect the interest of his donors - not his constituents. We need representatives who answer only to voters.

If either the Democrats or Republicans actually nominate someone who sincerely supports these policies, I'll drop out. But in the meantime, let's make our voices heard and tell the political establishment we are done accepting the status quo.
